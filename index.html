In plain English

What is email spoofing, and why should you care?

Email was built to be trusting. Unless your domain is set up to prove who really sent a message, anyone can send email that looks exactly like it came from you. Not a lookalike address, your actual company address. That's how fake invoices reach your customers. Three settings on your domain stop it, and this is what they do:

SPF, the guest list

Names the mail servers allowed to send email as your company. A message from anywhere else fails the check.

DKIM, the wax seal

Stamps every email you send with a seal only your server can make. If someone fakes or alters the message, the seal breaks.

DMARC, the doorman's orders

Tells the world's mail servers what to do with fakes: block them. It also reports back who's been trying to impersonate you.

Most small business domains have none of these, which is why impersonation scams work. Setting them up is a one-time job. If you'd like to know whether yours are in place, send me your domain and I'll check it for free.

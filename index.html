Case studies

Real problems, measured fixes

All work below was done on live production systems. Client names withheld; every result is verifiable on request.

Stopping a live email spoofing attack

Problem

A corporate domain was being used in a phishing campaign. Forensics showed two separate attack routes: external spoofing of the domain, and abuse of the website's own contact forms to send mail through the server.

What I did

Wrote a custom Exim ACL to block the external spoofing at the mail server, rerouted all website form submissions to an isolated mailbox, and reinforced reCAPTCHA across every form integration on the site.

Both attack routes closed. No recurrence since.

Email authentication across five production domains

Problem

A group of companies running five domains on shared infrastructure needed their email trustworthy: no spoofing, no legitimate mail landing in spam.

What I did

Configured SPF, DKIM and DMARC on every domain, aligned the DNS records with the actual sending sources, and tested inbox placement against Gmail, Outlook and Yahoo before signing off.

All five domains authenticated. Business mail lands in the inbox.

WordPress performance remediation

Problem

A corporate WordPress site scored 57 on Google PageSpeed mobile. Slow loads, heavy images, no caching strategy.

What I did

Phase one: server-level compression and cache headers, configured through cPanel and the web server directly rather than stacking plugins. Phases two and three (image conversion to WebP and page-builder restructuring) are in progress.

Mobile score 57 → 63 from phase one alone, verified in PageSpeed reports.

Multi-domain cPanel/WHM administration

Problem

Five live domains for a fintech group, one server, one administrator: me. Uptime, SSL, backups, PHP versioning and security are all a daily responsibility, not a one-off project.

What I did

Ongoing administration through WHM: SSH and firewall hardening, SSL across all sites, scheduled backups with sensible retention, DNS management, and documentation so the setup survives without me.

Five production domains, running clean.
